History became interesting to me when I read a biography about Cicero - the brilliant orator and politician of the ancient Roman republic. Since then I've spent time learning about the ancient Roman Republic and Empire, and the 19th and 20th centuries.

The Roman Republic and Empire gives us example after example of idealized characters from whom we can learn about different philosophies, morals, personalities, and rhetorical strategies.

More modern history teaches us how our world ended up how it is today. How some systems were set up by different groups for conscious purposes, and how other systems are historical accidents that we have grown to accept without thinking to much about them.
